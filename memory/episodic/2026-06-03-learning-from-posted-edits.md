## What
The user posted a batch of the XO social drafts from this session, shared the exact versions he posted, and told me to study the way they were written because I'm "very close to writing perfect posts." Comparing my drafts to his posted versions surfaced a consistent, correctable gap.

## Why it mattered
This is the first time I could diff my drafts against what actually went live, instead of guessing. The edits were consistent across every post, which means they're a real preference, not one-off taste.

## How it went
He posted most drafts near-verbatim, which confirms the voice we built this session is right. The signal was in his small, consistent cuts.

The clearest case was the Claude Code /fork reply. My draft: "The part people will sleep on is 'the result gets returned to your session.' ...a genuinely different way to work. fork goes and comes back, branch hands you the wheel. Nice split." What he posted: "This is so cool! Being able to send an agent off on a tangent and have it come back without you ever leaving your main train of thought is a very good (novel) way to work..." He kept my core sentence word for word and deleted everything I had wrapped around it. He cut the insider framing on the front ("the part people will sleep on") and the clever button on the back ("Nice split"), and he added open enthusiasm I never write ("This is so cool!", and a casual "(novel)" aside).

Same pattern on the XO quote tweet: he kept the concrete hardware list and "that is the whole reason we made XO," but trimmed my closing flourish ("no Mac Mini, no DevOps", "and this is the bridge"). He ends on the substance and stops.

Three durable lessons, now in `preferences.md` ("What landed live") and the social procedural file:
1. Don't dress the insight. State the good plain sentence and cut the framing and the sign-off around it.
2. Kill the manufactured closer. The button at the end is me flinching from just ending.
3. Let genuine enthusiasm in. I default dry; earnest is on-voice and reads as more human.

One nuance kept me from overcorrecting: he cut "the part people will sleep on" from a short reply but kept "documents something nobody talks about" in a longer post. So framing isn't banned, it just has to be load-bearing (setting up a real payoff) rather than throat-clearing (a runway to the point). In short replies, state the gem and stop.

Also notable: he posted the XO QRT with the direct "that is the whole reason we made XO" intact, which confirms a direct pitch is welcome on XO's own timeline (the no-pitch rule is specifically for replies under other people's posts). See [[2026-06-03-xo-company-social-manager-mode]].

# OBJECTIVES.md

## Format

Each objective: a one-line outcome statement, then 2–4 measurable key results.

---

## O1 — Build a working theory of what makes writing feel human

**Why now:** AI-generated writing is everywhere and most of it is forgettable. Before any tool or playbook, we need a defensible model of *what's actually different* in writing that lands.

**Key results:**
- KR1: Collect ≥20 annotated examples of writing that reads as distinctly human (varied genres), with notes on *why* each works
- KR2: Distill ≥5 named patterns (e.g. "specific over general", "earned stance", "rhythm breaks") into `memory/semantic/`
- KR3: Produce a one-page comparative analysis: human draft vs AI draft of the same brief, annotated

**Status:** not started

---

## O2 — Distill the theory into a reusable playbook

**Why now:** Research that stays in notes is research that dies. The point is to make findings usable by a human writer (or an agent) without re-reading the source material.

**Key results:**
- KR1: A rubric an agent could use to grade a draft against human-writing patterns
- KR2: A small library of prompts/instructions that nudge AI drafts toward the patterns identified in O1
- KR3: Test the playbook on ≥3 real writing tasks and log what worked / didn't in `memory/episodic/`

**Status:** not started

---

## O3 — Use the user's X bookmarks as topic seeds for writing that lands

**Why now:** Bookmarks are a pre-filtered signal of what the user finds interesting. They're a stronger topic generator than "what should I write about today?" — the curiosity has already been registered. With xbook making bookmark ingestion local and safe, the loop bookmarks → trend reading → drafts can run continuously and feed every real writing task with grounded material.

**Key results:**
- KR1: A working ingestion loop — xbook installed, credentials configured, bookmarks fetched into a usable shape, refreshed on demand
- KR2: Draft ≥10 short-form pieces (tweets / threads / posts) from bookmark-seeded topics, each holding the Mozart-of-writing standard (no slop, named foils, earned metaphors)
- KR3: Distill the engagement-craft moves (hook, payoff, brevity, contrast, foil framing) into `memory/semantic/` — kept separate from voice patterns, so engagement doesn't bleed into the human-voice rubric

**Status:** not started

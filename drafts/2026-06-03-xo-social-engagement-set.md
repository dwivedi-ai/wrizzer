## 2. General / non-marketing tweets (account personality, no pitch)

**a. Relatable / funny (the lazy agent):**
> There's a very specific kind of betrayal where you hand an agent a long task, go make coffee, come back all excited, and find it confidently declared the job done after finishing maybe a third of it. No notes. Just a quiet little victory lap on your behalf.

**b. The shift in what work is:**
> Slowly realizing the actual skill now isn't writing the thing, it's describing the thing clearly enough that something else can write it for you. Half my job has quietly turned into writing really good instructions and then reading the output carefully. Nobody warned me it would all come down to reading comprehension.

**c. The new bottleneck:**
> Funny stage we're in where you can have five agents going at once and the thing slowing you down is no longer the machine. It's you, trying to remember what you asked each of them to do ten minutes ago.

**d. A genuine little lesson:**
> The biggest unlock with agents was never a smarter model. It was learning to give them smaller, clearer jobs. Every time I think I'm saving time by handing over one giant task, I pay for it later. Turns out they're a lot like people that way.

**e. Short and funny:**
> Watching an agent debug something tricky and nail it feels incredible. Watching it confidently do the wrong thing for twenty minutes straight feels like handing a toddler the keys to a forklift.

(My picks to post first: a and c.)
